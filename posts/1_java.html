Quick introduction about the course:
Programming one (or ID1018) was the first course in programming I took in my life. The intention of the course is to introduce object-oriented programming with both a series of lectures and a bunch of programming assignments. The intended goals of the course is to provide knowledge on the fundamental design of programming (basic syntax, fundamental data types, basic I/O, ...), simple data-structures (arrays, strings, variable passing, string processing, pointers, ...), as well as providing us with a first experience on solving alorithmic problems. A great deal of the course was the theory about object-oriented programming, where concepts as 'encapsulation', 'inheritance', 'instance', 'class' or 'polymorphism' and so on where explained in depth. Last but not least we got aquainted with throwing and catching errors, which at first sounded a bit useless, but is good to know in the long run.
I will leave the exercises below as well as the code I wrote to solve them. It may not be the best code, but I always pride myself on keeping a neat presentation and some explanatory comments.
